Continuous diffusion models

We are familiar with the discrete formulation,

Xk+1=Xk+γXk+2γZk+1,Zk+1∼N(0,I),X_{k+1} = X_k + \gamma X_k + \sqrt{2\gamma}Z_{k+1}, \quad Z_{k+1} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I), Xk+1​=Xk​+γXk​+2γ​Zk+1​,Zk+1​∼N(0,I),

is just the Euler-Maruyama discretization of the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process,

dXt=−Xtdt+2dBt,dX_t = -X_t dt + \sqrt{2} dB_t, dXt​=−Xt​dt+2​dBt​,

where BtB_tBt​ is the standard Brownian motion.
The OU process has a closed form solution,

Xt=e−tX0+1−e−2tZ,Z∼N(0,I).X_t = e^{-t}X_0 + \sqrt{1-e^{-2t}}Z, \quad Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I). Xt​=e−tX0​+1−e−2t​Z,Z∼N(0,I).

Therefore, it converges exponentially fast to the standard Gaussian distribution regardless of the initial distribution of X0X_0X0​.

Let XtX_tXt​ be the solution of the SDE above, and define a time-reversed process Yt=XT−tY_t = X_{T-t}Yt​=XT−t​. Then YtY_tYt​ satisfies the reverse-time SDE,

dYt=(Yt+2∇log⁡pT−t(Yt))dt+2dBˉt,dY_t = \left(Y_t + 2\nabla \log p_{T-t}(Y_t)\right)dt + \sqrt{2}d\bar{B}_t, dYt​=(Yt​+2∇logpT−t​(Yt​))dt+2​dBˉt​,

where Bˉt\bar{B}_tBˉt​ is another standard Brownian motion, and ptp_tpt​ is the density of XtX_tXt​. This is a special case of the general result by Anderson (1982). Modern score-based generative models (SGM) use a neural network to approximate the score function ∇log⁡pt\nabla \log p_t∇logpt​ in the equivalent discretized form:

Yk+1=Yk+γ(Yk+2sθ(Yk,T−kγ))+2γZk+1,Zk+1∼N(0,I).Y_{k+1} = Y_k + \gamma\left(Y_k + 2s_\theta(Y_k, T-k\gamma)\right) + \sqrt{2\gamma}Z_{k+1}, \quad Z_{k+1} \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I). Yk+1​=Yk​+γ(Yk​+2sθ​(Yk​,T−kγ))+2γ​Zk+1​,Zk+1​∼N(0,I).

The data distribution is denoted as π\piπ.

Theorem: Convergence of diffusion models (De Bortoli et al. 2021): Assume the score function estimator is well-trained, i.e., exists M≥0M \geq 0M≥0 such that for all t∈[0,T]t \in [0, T]t∈[0,T],

∥sθ∗(x,t)−∇log⁡pt(x)∥2≤M.\Vert s_{\theta^*}(x, t) - \nabla \log p_t(x) \Vert_2 \leq M. ∥sθ∗​(x,t)−∇logpt​(x)∥2​≤M.

Then, there exist B,C,D≥0B, C, D \geq 0B,C,D≥0 such that for all N∈NN \in \mathbb{N}N∈N and T=NγT = N\gammaT=Nγ, the following holds:

∥L(YN)−π∥TV≤Be−T+C(M+γ1/2)eDT.\Vert \mathcal{L}(Y_N) - \pi \Vert_{\text{TV}} \leq B e^{-T} + C(M + \gamma^{1/2})e^{DT}. ∥L(YN​)−π∥TV​≤Be−T+C(M+γ1/2)eDT.

What does the theorem say?

  1. If the score function estimator is perfect, i.e., M=0M=0M=0, then as T→∞T \to \inftyT→∞, the generated distribution converges to the data distribution π\piπ.
  2. If the score function estimator is imperfect, i.e., M>0M>0M>0, then as T→∞T \to \inftyT→∞, the generated distribution diverges from the data distribution π\piπ.
