Positional Embeddings in Transformers

Posted on 2025-05-07 In Generative models

Positional Embeddings in Vasvani et al. (2017)

The original Transformer model proposed by Vasvani et al. (2017) uses sinusoidal positional embeddings. The idea is to encode the position of each token in the sequence using a combination of sine and cosine functions with different frequencies. The formula for the positional embedding is as follows:

PE(pos,2i)=sin⁡(pos100002idmodel)PE(pos,2i+1)=cos⁡(pos100002idmodel)\begin{aligned} PE_{(pos, 2i)} &= \sin\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{\frac{2i}{d_{model}}}}\right) \\ PE_{(pos, 2i+1)} &= \cos\left(\frac{pos}{10000^{\frac{2i}{d_{model}}}}\right) \end{aligned} PE(pos,2i)​PE(pos,2i+1)​​=sin(10000dmodel​2i​pos​)=cos(10000dmodel​2i​pos​)​

where pospospos is the position of the token in the sequence, iii is the dimension index, and dmodeld_{model}dmodel​ is the dimensionality of the model. The sine and cosine functions are used to ensure that each position has a unique representation, and the different frequencies allow the model to learn relationships between tokens at different distances.

Relative Positional Embeddings: Shaw et al. (2018) / Huang et al. (2018)

The key idea of relative positional embeddings is to encode the relative distances between tokens rather than their absolute positions. They are computed on the fly during the attention computation:

ei,j=xiWQ(xjWK+ai,jK)Tdke_{i,j} = \frac{x_i W^Q(x_j W^K + a^K_{i,j})^T}{\sqrt{d_k}} ei,j​=dk​​xi​WQ(xj​WK+ai,jK​)T​

αi,j=exp⁡(ei,j)∑k=1nexp⁡(ei,k)\alpha_{i,j} = \frac{\exp(e_{i,j})}{\sum_{k=1}^{n} \exp(e_{i,k})}αi,j​=∑k=1n​exp(ei,k​)exp(ei,j​)​

The output of the attention layer is computed as:

zi=∑j=1nαi,j(xjWV+ai,jV)z_i = \sum_{j=1}^{n} \alpha_{i,j} (x_j W^V + a^V_{i,j})zi​=j=1∑n​αi,j​(xj​WV+ai,jV​)

where ai,jKa^K_{i,j}ai,jK​ and ai,jVa^V_{i,j}ai,jV​ are the relative positional embeddings for the key and value respectively. The relative positional embeddings are learned during training and allow the model to focus on tokens that are close to each other in the sequence.

AliBi: Attention with Linear Biases (Beltagy et al. 2020)

The key idea is to use a linear bias term that depends on the distance between tokens, rather than using learned embeddings. The bias term is similar in the relative positional embeddings, but it is computed using a linear function of the distance between tokens.

This is very easy to implement and can extended to longer sequences.

RoPE: Rotary Positional Embedding (Su et al. 2021)

The key idea is to rotate the query and key representations with an angle proportional to their absolute positions before applying the dot product attention.

Relative Position Bias in Swin Transformer (Liu et al. 2021)

Fourier Position Embedding (Hua et al. 2025)
